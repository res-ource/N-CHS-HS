Step 3: Joint, Marginal & Conditional Frequency

Let's learn some key terms for two-way tables.

Key Terms

  • Joint Frequency: An entry in the body of the table. It's where a row and column join.
  • Marginal Frequency: An entry in the "Total" row or "Total" column. They are in the margins of the table.

Conditional Frequency

This is the most common type. It finds a relative frequency for a specific subgroup or condition. You've already done this!

The "whole" is NOT the grand total. It's a marginal frequency (a row or column total) that matches the condition in the question.

Example: "Of the students who drive, what percent are seniors?" The condition is "students who drive," so the "whole" is the total number of drivers.
