Data Storyteller: Day 1

Step 1: First, a Quick Review

Before we design your project, let's quickly review the two main types of data you'll be working with: Quantitative and Qualitative.

Step 2: Two Types of Data

Quantitative

This is data that can be counted or measured.

  • It answers "how many?" or "how much?".
  • It's always a number.
  • Example: "8 hours" of sleep.

Qualitative (or Categorical)

This is data that can be sorted into groups or categories.

  • It answers "what kind?" or "which one?".
  • It's usually a word.
  • Example: "Winter" (as a favorite season).

Step 4: Let's Walk Through an Example

Great job on the quiz! Before you create your own project, let's look at a complete example. We'll call this one "My Reading Habits."

Step 5: Example Project Title

A good title is short and clear. This one tells us exactly what the project is about.

Project Title:

"My Reading Habits"

Step 6: Example Quantitative Question

This question asks for a number. Notice it's very specific ("pages per day") so it's easy to measure.

Quantitative Question:

"How many pages did I read each day?"

Step 7: Example Categorical Question

This question asks for a word or category. This will let us group the data (e.g., "I read 50 pages of 'Fantasy'").

Categorical Question:

"What genre was I reading?" (e.g., Fantasy, Sci-Fi, Non-Fiction)

Step 8: Now, It's Your Turn!

You've seen a full example. Now you're ready to define your own project. We'll walk through this one step at a time.

Step 9: Your Project Title

First, think of a clear title. This helps you focus. You'll enter this in a form at the end.

For example:

"A Study of Sleep Habits"

Step 10: The Quantitative Question

You need one question with a number answer. This asks "how much?" or "how many?".

For example:

"How many hours of sleep did you get last night?"

Step 11: The Categorical Question

You also need one question with a category or word answer. This asks "what kind?" or "which one?".

For example:

"What is your favorite season?"
