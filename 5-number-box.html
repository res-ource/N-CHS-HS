Step 2: What is the 5-Number Summary?

The 5-Number Summary is a set of five values that divide a data set into four equal parts. It gives you a great snapshot of your data's spread and center.

The five numbers are:

  1. Minimum (Min): The smallest value in the data set.
  2. Quartile 1 (Q1): The 25th percentile. This is the median of the *lower half* of the data.
  3. Median (Q2): The 50th percentile. This is the middle value of the entire data set.
  4. Quartile 3 (Q3): The 75th percentile. This is the median of the *upper half* of the data.
  5. Maximum (Max): The largest value in the data set.

Step 3: How to Find It (Part 1)

Task 1: Sort the Data

You cannot find the 5-Number Summary without first sorting your data.

Rule: Always arrange your data set in order from smallest to largest (ascending order).

Example:

Unsorted Data:

8, 3, 5, 1, 9, 6, 2

Sorted Data:

1, 2, 3, 5, 6, 8, 9

Step 4: How to Find It (Part 2)

Task 2: Find the Minimum and Maximum

This is the easiest part! Once your data is sorted:

  • The Minimum is the first number in your list.
  • The Maximum is the last number in your list.

Example:

Sorted Data:

1, 2, 3, 5, 6, 8, 9

Minimum: 1
Maximum: 9

Step 5: How to Find It (Part 3)

Task 3: Find the Median (Q2)

The Median is the value right in the middle of the sorted data.

Sorted Data:

1, 2, 3, 5, 6, 8, 9

In this set, the number 5 is in the middle. There are three numbers before it and three numbers after it.

Median (Q2): 5

What if there's an even number of data points?

If there are two middle numbers, you find the average (mean) of them.

Example (Even Set): 1, 2, 4, 6, 8, 9

The middle numbers are 4 and 6.
(4 + 6) / 2 = 10 / 2 = 5.
The Median for this set would be 5.

Step 6: How to Find It (Part 4)

Task 4: Find Q1 and Q3

Now you just find the median of the lower and upper halves of the data.

Sorted Data:

[ 1, 2, 3 ] , 5 , [ 6, 8, 9 ]

  • Quartile 1 (Q1): This is the median of the lower half (the numbers before the median 5). The lower half is [1, 2, 3]. The middle number is 2.
  • Quartile 3 (Q3): This is the median of the upper half (the numbers after the median 5). The upper half is [6, 8, 9]. The middle number is 8.
Q1: 2
Q3: 8

(Note: When the data set is odd, you do *not* include the median when finding Q1 and Q3.)

Step 7: The Final 5-Number Summary

Let's review what we found for our data set: [1, 2, 3, 5, 6, 8, 9]

Minimum Q1 Median (Q2) Q3 Maximum
1 2 5 8 9

That's it! This summary is the "recipe" for building a Box Plot.

Step 8: What is a Box Plot?

A Box Plot (or Box-and-Whisker Plot) is a visual representation of the 5-Number Summary. It's extremely useful for seeing the data's spread and shape at a glance.

Here is what one looks like, built from our example summary [1, 2, 5, 8, 9]:

  • The "box" is drawn from Q1 to Q3.
  • A line inside the box marks the Median.
  • The "whiskers" (lines) extend from the box out to the Minimum and Maximum.

Step 9: Analyzing the Shape (Symmetry)

A Box Plot makes it easy to see if your data is symmetric or skewed.

Symmetric

The median is in the middle of the box, and the whiskers are about the same length. The data is evenly spread.

Step 10: Analyzing the Shape (Skew)

Skewed Left

The median is closer to the right (Q3). The left whisker is longer, meaning the data has a "tail" of low values.

Skewed Right

The median is closer to the left (Q1). The right whisker is longer, meaning the data has a "tail" of high values.
